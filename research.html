The Consequences of Gentrification on Incumbent Residents
Mohamed Amine Derdari and Emma Duchini

This paper studies the causal effects of gentrification on incumbent residents in England over 2001–2021, using Lower Layer Super Output Areas (LSOAs) as the unit of analysis. I instrument for gentrification—measured as the increase in university-educated residents in initially low-educated neighbourhoods—using the density of pre-2001 listed buildings from Historic England's National Heritage List. The analysis harmonises census data to a common 2001 geography to avoid endogeneity from boundary changes. Upcoming results will examine the effects on incumbent children's education and crime outcomes using the MoJDfE linked dataset, as well as education and wage outcomes using the Longitudinal Education Outcomes (LEO) data.

Understanding Gentrification through Urban Connectivity: A Machine Learning Approach
Mohamed Amine Derdari and Alessandro Venerandi

This project uses random forest models to explore the relationship between gentrification and measures of urban street-network connectivity, including straightness, betweenness, and localness. The goal is to uncover which dimensions of neighbourhood connectivity are most predictive of gentrification dynamics.

City Shape Variation and Pollution
Mohamed Amine Derdari, Moritz Hennicke and Alexis Mergan

This project investigates how variation in city shape affects pollution levels.
