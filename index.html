Mohamed Amine Derdari

PhD Candidate in Economics
Université Libre de Bruxelles (ECARES)

Welcome! I am a PhD candidate in Economics at the European Center for Advanced Research in Economics and Statistics. My research focuses on urban economics and spatial inequality, with a particular interest in how cities develop and how neighbourhood change shapes the outcomes of residents within them.

Prior to my PhD, I obtained two Master's degree and a Bachelor's degree in Economics from the Université Libre de Bruxelles.

I am currently a visiting researcher at the Department of Economics of the University of Essex (2025–2026), where I have access to the MoJDfE linked dataset to study neighbourhood effects on children's education and crime outcomes.
